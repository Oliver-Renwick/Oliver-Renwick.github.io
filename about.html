About

I'm a real-time graphics programmer with a strong interest in building high-performance rendering systems, physics simulations, and networked multiplayer experiences. I got into graphics programming because I’ve always been curious about how games really work under the hood — that curiosity led me deep into engines, shaders, and systems like Vulkan.

I also run a YouTube channel where I share devlogs, breakdowns of graphics techniques, and behind-the-scenes looks at how I build my game engine from scratch. It’s my way of giving back to the community and documenting my progress.

When I'm not coding, I’m usually deep into a good video game or reading something immersive — fiction, philosophy, anything with strong writing really. It helps me recharge and occasionally sparks new ideas for my projects.
